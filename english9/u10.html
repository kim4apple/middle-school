You're supposed to shake hands.

礼仪与文化

📖 话题：Customs and Culture💬 功能：Talk about customs and what you are supposed to do📚 语法：Be supposed to + V (应该/被期望做某事)📝 写作：Describe customs and etiquette in different countries

🎯 我能做到

📖 课文精讲 · You're supposed to shake hands.
场景：中国学生李华第一次去美国参加交换项目，向美国朋友Mike询问美国礼仪。
Li Hua:Mike, I'm a little nervous. What am I supposed to do when I meet someone for the first time?
Mike:You're supposed to shake hands. It's the most common greeting in the US.
Li Hua:What about eye contact? In China, it's not always polite to look directly at someone.
Mike:Oh, in the US, you're supposed to make eye contact. It shows you are confident and honest.
Li Hua:I see. And when I'm invited to dinner, am I supposed to arrive on time?
Mike:Yes, you're supposed to arrive on time. Being late is considered impolite.
Li Hua:What should I do at the table? I'm not supposed to make noise while eating, am I?
Mike:That's right. And you're supposed to say «excuse me» if you need to leave the table. Don't worry, you'll do great!
📖 查看翻译

Li Hua: 迈克，我有点紧张。我第一次见到别人时应该做什么？

Mike: 你应该握手。这是美国最常见的问候方式。

Li Hua: 那眼神接触呢？在中国，直视别人不总是礼貌的。

Mike: 哦，在美国，你应该有眼神交流。这显示你自信和诚实。

Li Hua: 我明白了。那当我被邀请吃饭时，我应该准时到吗？

Mike: 是的，你应该准时到。迟到被认为是不礼貌的。

Li Hua: 吃饭时我应该怎么做？我不应该在吃饭时发出声音，对吗？

Mike: 对。如果你需要离开餐桌，应该说「抱歉」。别担心，你会做得很好的！

🔑 重点讲解：

be supposed to do 表示「应该做某事；被期望做某事」— You are supposed to shake hands.

be not supposed to do 表示「不应该做某事」— You are not supposed to eat with your hands.

和 should 的区别：should 语气更强，be supposed to 语气较委婉。

不同国家礼仪不同：日本鞠躬、法国贴面、美国握手。

🔤 重点句型 · Sentence Patterns
主语 + be supposed to + 动词原形
表示「应该做某事」。
You are supposed to shake hands. / You are supposed to arrive on time.
主语 + be not supposed to + 动词原形
表示「不应该做某事」。
You are not supposed to eat with your hands. / We are not supposed to be late.
What + be + 主语 + supposed to do?
询问应该做什么。
What am I supposed to do? / What are we supposed to wear?
You're supposed to... / You should...
be supposed to 比 should 语气更委婉。
You're supposed to bow in Japan. / You should try the local food.
📚 语法聚焦 · Be supposed to 结构

be supposed to 的用法

形式用法含义例句
肯定句be supposed to + V应该You are supposed to shake hands.
否定句be not supposed to + V不应该You are not supposed to be late.
疑问句Be + 主语 + supposed to + V?应该……吗？Am I supposed to bring a gift?

be supposed to vs should

表达语气用法例句
be supposed to较委婉表示惯例或期望You're supposed to bow in Japan.
should较强表示义务或建议You should study hard.

be supposed to 中的 be 动词要随主语变化（am/is/are）。

be supposed to 的否定式在 be 后加 not。

be supposed to 的疑问式把 be 动词提前。

When in Rome, do as the Romans do. 入乡随俗。

📖 阅读专项 · Customs Around the World

Different countries have different customs. What is polite in one country might be impolite in another. It's important to learn about these customs before you travel.

In Japan, you are supposed to bow when you greet someone. Bowing shows respect. You are also supposed to remove your shoes before entering someone's home. In Japan, it's considered impolite to tip at restaurants.

In France, you are supposed to kiss on the cheek when you greet friends. The number of kisses varies by region. In France, it's polite to say «bonjour» when you enter a store. You are supposed to keep your hands on the table during meals.

In Korea, you are supposed to use both hands when giving something to an older person. It shows respect. You are also supposed to look away when drinking with someone older.

Learning these customs can help you avoid misunderstandings and show respect for other cultures. Remember: when in Rome, do as the Romans do.

Table manners are different around the world. In China, you are supposed to wait for the oldest person to start eating. You are not supposed to stick your chopsticks into your food. In Western countries, you are supposed to keep your napkin on your lap. You are supposed to say «excuse me» if you need to leave the table. In some Middle Eastern countries, you are supposed to eat with your right hand. The left hand is considered unclean.

1 A. What are Chinese supposed to do before eating? B. Why should you wait for the oldest person? C. How to use chopsticks correctly? D. What is the Chinese dining etiquette?
2 A. Keep your napkin on your lap. B. Keep your napkin on the table. C. Don't use a napkin. D. Fold the napkin.
3 A. The right hand. B. The left hand. C. Both hands. D. Chopsticks.
答案
1. Wait for the oldest person to start eating.；2. A；3. A

China has many customs that visitors should know. When you meet someone for the first time in China, you are supposed to shake hands. A light handshake is polite.
